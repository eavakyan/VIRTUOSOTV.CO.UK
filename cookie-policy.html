Cookie Policy

Last Updated: December 25, 2024

This Cookie Policy explains how VUGA Media Group LLC ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website virtuosotv.co.uk ("Website"). This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period of time or until you delete them.
  • Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for various purposes, including to:

  • Enable essential website functionality
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our website
  • Improve your browsing experience
  • Analyze website traffic and performance
  • Deliver relevant content and advertisements

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences in several ways:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically find these options in the "Settings," "Preferences," or "Privacy" menu of your browser. Here are links to cookie management instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of targeted advertising from participating companies through:

  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI)
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA)
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA)

Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

6. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable or reject cookies, please note that:

  • Some parts of our website may not function properly
  • You may not be able to access certain features
  • Your preferences and settings may not be saved
  • You may see less relevant advertisements

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that sends a signal to websites requesting that your browsing not be tracked. Our website currently does not respond to DNT signals. However, you can manage your privacy preferences through your browser's cookie settings as described above.
